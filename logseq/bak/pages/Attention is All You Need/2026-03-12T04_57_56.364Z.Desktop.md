- The core of the Transformer consists of the **encorder** and **decorder**.
- # Self-Attention
	- The core mechanism of self-attention is defined by the equation $$\text{Attention}(Q, K, V) = \text{softmax}\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d_k}}\right)V$$
	- **Q (Query)**: A learned projection of the token seeking contextual information from other positions.
	- **K (Key)**: A learned projection of all tokens, used to compute compatibility scores with the query.
	- **V (Value)**: A learned projection of all tokens, representing the actual information to be aggregated.
		- The separation of K and V is crucial: a token may have high similarity (via K) to the query, yet the relevant information for the task comes from V. For instance, when querying "apple" about its "color," the word "red" may not score highest in key-similarity, but its value representation carries the desired attribute.
	- ## Example: "The cat sat on the mat because it was tired"
		- When processing **"it"**, the model asks: "who am I referring to?"
		- **Query** (from "it"): "I need to find what I refer to"
		- **Key** (from each word): each word advertises what it represents — "cat" says "I'm a noun/subject"
		- **Attention score**: dot product of Query × Keys → "cat" scores high
		- **Value** (from "cat"): carries the actual semantic meaning of "cat" into the representation of "it"

- # Cross-Attention
- # Encorder
	- The **encorder** is a two-layer structure comprising a multi-head attention layer followed by a position-wise feed forward network. This structure is repeated N times with residual connections.
- # Decorder
	- The **decorder** shares a similar architecture but includes two additional layers: a linear projection and a softmax function.
		- In the **decorder**, two attention mechanisms are employed.
		- The first is masked self-attention, which embeds the output tokens and project them into high-dimensional space.
		- The second is cross-attention, which computes the relationship between the encorder's representation and the decorder's self-attention outputs.
- # Output word
	- The final two layers map the high dimensional representation back to the vocabulary space producing a probability distribution for next-token prediction.
